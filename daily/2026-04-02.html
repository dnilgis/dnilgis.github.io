🧠 THE MORE YOU KNOW
Why Hog Price Explosions Happen
Today's 22% hog rally illustrates how thin livestock markets can become. Unlike grains with global production buffers, protein markets operate on razor-thin margins with limited storage. A single disease outbreak, processing plant closure, or export surge can instantly flip supply/demand balance. The hog cycle typically runs 2-3 years from breeding decisions to market, so today's shortage often reflects decisions made when prices were much lower. This volatility is why successful livestock producers focus on risk management, not just production efficiency.
