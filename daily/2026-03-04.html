🧠 THE MORE YOU KNOW
Why Crude Rallies Hurt Corn More Than Help
Every farmer knows higher crude should boost ethanol demand and corn prices. But that's only half the equation. A 3% crude jump adds roughly 7¢ per gallon to diesel costs and pressures fertilizer prices through natural gas. For corn, the net effect is often negative — especially pre-plant when input purchasing peaks. The ethanol boost takes months to materialize, but the input cost hit happens immediately.
