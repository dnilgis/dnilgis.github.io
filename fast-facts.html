Corn Pests
Black CutwormCorn3 or more cut plants per 100V1–V5Monitor after adult flights. Cut plants at/below growing point = dead plant.
Corn Rootworm (adult)Corn0.75 beetles per plant + silk clipping <1/2"VT–R1Silk clipping at pollination is the critical damage window.
European Corn BorerCorn1 egg mass or 1 plant with fresh feeding per 5 plantsV8–VTBt hybrids provide good control. Scout non-Bt refuge strips.
Western Bean CutwormCorn8% egg mass infestationPre-tasselTargets ear tip and kernels. WBC maps at extension sites.
Japanese BeetleCorn3+ beetles/ear, silk <1/2" with poor pollinationVT–R1Treat only during active pollination (4–5 days).

Soybean AphidSoybean250 aphids/plant on ≥80% of plants, increasingV4–R5Count 20 plants per field. Only treat if population increasing & before R5.
Bean Leaf BeetleSoybean20–30% defoliationV stagesPod feeding at R3–R5 can warrant action at lower thresholds.
Japanese Beetle (soybean)Soybean20% defoliation before bloom; 15% during/afterV–R6Canopy closure reduces access — focus on field edges.
Soybean Gall MidgeSoybeanAny presence — no established ETV1–R2Emerging pest in MN/WI. Orange larvae at stem base. Scout field edges.
