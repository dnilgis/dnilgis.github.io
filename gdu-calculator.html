📐 GDU FORMULA & METHODOLOGY

Corn GDU Formula
GDU/day = ((min(Hi, 86°F) + max(Lo, 50°F)) ÷ 2) − 50

Temperature caps:
• Max daily high capped at 86°F (above this, corn growth doesn't increase)
• Min daily low floored at 50°F (below this, no heat accumulation)
Soybean GDU Formula
Soybeans use the same base formula but are photoperiod-sensitive — flowering is triggered by day length (short-day plant), not GDUs alone.

GDU to R1 varies by maturity group. MG 2.0–4.0 typical in Midwest = ~800–1200 GDUs to first flower.
RM × GDU Rule of Thumb
Corn: GDUs to black layer ≈ (RM × 9) + 375
Example: 108-day hybrid ≈ (108 × 9) + 375 = 1,347 GDUs to black layer

Always verify with the seed company's published GDU requirements for your specific hybrid.
Limitations
• GDUs are a guide, not a guarantee
• Soil temp at planting affects early accumulation
• Cold snaps can reset or delay stage progress
• Hybrid differences of ±100–200 GDUs are common
• Use NOAA or your local extension office for actual daily GDU data
